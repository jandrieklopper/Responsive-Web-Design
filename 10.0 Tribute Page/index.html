Marie Curie

Pioneering physicist and chemist, the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.

Here's a time line of Marie Curie's life:

    1867 - Birth and Early Life
  • November 7, 1867: Marie Sk≈Çodowska Curie is born in Warsaw, Poland, then part of the Russian Empire.
    • 1891 - Move to Paris
  • Marie moves to Paris to pursue higher education, enrolling at the Sorbonne (University of Paris).
    • 1895 - Marriage to Pierre Curie
  • Marie marries Pierre Curie, a fellow scientist, on July 26, 1895.
    • 1897 - Birth of First Daughter
  • Irene Curie, the first daughter of Marie and Pierre, is born.
    • 1898 - Discovery of Polonium and Radium
  • Marie and Pierre Curie discover the elements Polonium and Radium.
    • 1903 - Nobel Prize in Physics
  • Marie Curie shares the Nobel Prize in Physics with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their work on radioactivity.
    • 1906 - Widowed
  • Pierre Curie dies in a tragic accident. Marie continues their scientific work.
    • 1911 - Nobel Prize in Chemistry
  • Marie Curie receives the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her discoveries of radium and polonium, becoming the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.
    • 1914 - World War I
  • Marie Curie establishes mobile radiography units, or "Little Curies," to provide X-rays for medical treatment during World War I.
    • 1921 - USA Tour
  • Marie Curie tours the United States, where she receives a warm welcome and support for her scientific contributions.
    • 1934 - Death
  • Marie Curie dies on July 4, 1934, in Passy, France, due to complications from exposure to radiation.
    • Legacy
  • Marie Curie's contributions to science and her pioneering research in radioactivity continue to inspire generations of scientists and women in STEM.
